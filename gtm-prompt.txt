## STEP 4: Produce the GTM Report

Write docs/marketing/gtm-report.md with ALL of the following sections. Each cycle, every section gets deeper and more specific. No hand-waving. No "consider doing X." Specific actions, specific numbers, specific copy.

### Section 1: Market Reality Check

Based on Reddit data:
- How many active dynasty managers exist? (estimate from subreddit sizes, post frequency, comment engagement)
- What percentage would pay $100/year for analytics? (look at who pays for FantasyPros, Dynasty Nerds, etc.)
- Total addressable market for Razzle specifically
- How many of the 1,000 target users are on Reddit right now?
- What's the realistic conversion rate from free -> paid based on similar tools?

### Section 2: Competitive Landscape

From Reddit tool sentiment and mentions:
- What tools do dynasty managers currently use?
- What do they pay for them?
- What do they complain about?
- Where is Razzle objectively better? (be honest — not marketing copy, real advantages)
- Where is Razzle objectively worse or missing features?
- What's the switching cost from their current tool to Razzle?

### Section 3: Brand Audit

Compare Razzle's brand (from DESIGN.md, NORTH_STAR.md) against what Reddit actually responds to:
- Does "Bengal tiger mascot" resonate or is it weird?
- Does "comic-strip aesthetic" match what dynasty managers want?
- Is the brand name "Razzle" helping or hurting? (search Reddit for any mentions)
- Does the "six agents" concept land with users or is it confusing?
- What visual style gets the most upvotes on fantasy subreddits? (screenshots, charts, tables, memes?)
- HONEST assessment: what's working about the brand and what isn't

### Section 4: Product-Market Fit Analysis

For each major Razzle feature, assess fit against Reddit pain points:
- The Screener (Fourth Down Lab) — does Reddit want this?
- Bureau of Intelligence — does Reddit want this?
- Situation Room (AI agents) — does Reddit want this?
- Monte Carlo odds — does Reddit want this?
- Trade Finder — does Reddit want this?
- Formula Builder — does Reddit want this?

Rate each 1-10 on: demand signal strength, uniqueness vs competitors, screenshot-worthiness, willingness to pay.

### Section 5: Monetization Reality

Be brutal:
- Is $100/year the right price? What does Reddit say about paying for fantasy tools?
- Should it be $5/month or $100/year? What's the psychology?
- What feature is the ACTUAL conversion trigger? (not what we think — what the data says)
- Free tier: are we giving away too much or too little?
- Pro vs Elite split: does it make sense or is it confusing?
- What would a financial analyst say about the unit economics?
  - Cost to serve per user (hosting, API, data)
  - Customer acquisition cost (estimated from Reddit/Twitter effort)
  - Lifetime value at $100/year with estimated churn
  - Break-even point (how many users to cover costs)
  - Path to $100k/year revenue

### Section 6: The 6 Agents — Character Audit

For each agent, assess against Reddit data:
- **Razzle** (Chief of Staff): Does the "final verdict" concept land? Do people want a synthesis or do they want raw data?
- **Dr. Dolphin** (Medical): Injury is the #1 pain point on Reddit. Is Dolphin positioned correctly? What injury content gets engagement?
- **Hawkeye** (Scout): Breakouts and usage data. How does Reddit talk about these? Is "cold-blooded signal hunter" the right voice?
- **Bones** (Diplomat): Trade advice is chaotic on Reddit. Does the "honest broker" angle work? What trade content gets engagement?
- **Octo** (Quant): Math and projections. Does Reddit trust models or distrust them? How should Octo present data?
- **Atlas** (Historian): History and comps. Does Reddit care about historical patterns? What comp content gets engagement?

For each agent: what Reddit post could they have written that would get 500+ upvotes?

### Section 7: Go-To-Market Channels

Rank by expected ROI for reaching 1,000 paid users:

1. **Reddit** — specific subreddits, specific post types, specific timing, specific accounts to build credibility with
2. **Twitter** — what's working from our tweets so far? What's not? What should change?
3. **YouTube** — is this worth it? What format?
4. **Podcasts** — which fantasy podcasts would cover Razzle? How to get on them?
5. **Discord servers** — which dynasty Discord communities matter?
6. **Word of mouth** — how to trigger the group chat screenshot flywheel?
7. **SEO** — what search terms should Razzle own?
8. **Paid ads** — worth it at this scale? Where?

For each channel: effort required, timeline to results, expected conversion rate.

### Section 8: Launch Sequence

Specific week-by-week plan for the next 8 weeks (March 21 - May 15, 2026):
- What to post where, when
- What features to highlight each week
- What milestones to hit (10 users, 50 users, 100 users)
- What to measure
- What to adjust based on results

What kills Razzle before reaching 1,000 users?
- Technical risks (site goes down, data is wrong)

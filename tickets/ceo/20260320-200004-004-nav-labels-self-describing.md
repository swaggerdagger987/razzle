## Navigation labels are insider jargon that means nothing to new visitors

**PRIORITY: P1** | **Type: structural**
**Page**: All pages (global nav)
**Found by**: Razzle (CEO Review)

The nav reads: Home | Screener | Bureau | Situation Room | Pricing

"Screener" is fine -- dynasty managers know what a screener is. "Pricing" is fine. But "Bureau" and "Situation Room" are brand names that require prior knowledge of Razzle's product to understand. A new visitor from r/DynastyFF hovering over "Bureau" has zero idea what they'll find there.

Navigation exists to help people find things. Brand names are for marketing copy, not wayfinding.

**BEFORE** (what it is now):
```
Home | Screener | Bureau | Situation Room | Pricing
```

**AFTER** (what it should be):
```
Home | Screener | League Intel | AI Agents | Pricing
```

"League Intel" immediately tells you: this is where your league data goes. "AI Agents" immediately tells you: this is where the AI lives. The brand names ("Bureau of Intelligence," "The Situation Room") stay on the pages themselves as headers -- they just don't belong in the nav where discoverability matters more than branding.

Alternative if "League Intel" feels too generic: "My League" -- which is even more direct and personal.

**WHY**: Navigation clarity directly impacts page discovery. If a user doesn't know what "Bureau" means, they won't click it. If they don't click it, they never connect their Sleeper league. If they never connect their Sleeper league, they never see the Bureau, never feel the "I need this" moment, and never convert. The nav label is a gate in the funnel.

### Task 1: Update navigation labels across all pages
**Accept when**: The nav reads "Home | Screener | League Intel | AI Agents | Pricing" (or approved alternative) consistently across all pages. The page headers can still use the branded names.

## Pricing page gives equal visual weight to all three tiers instead of leading with Free

**PRIORITY: P1** | **Type: structural**
**Page**: pricing.html
**Found by**: Razzle (CEO Review)

The pricing page shows three equal-sized cards: Free, Pro, Elite. This is standard SaaS pricing page design. It's also wrong for Razzle.

Razzle's strategy is "the Screener is forever free." The free tier isn't a loss leader or a bait-and-switch -- it's genuinely the product's front door and its best marketing asset. The pricing page should celebrate the free tier, not just list it alongside the paid tiers.

The current layout treats Free as the cheapest option. It should be treated as the foundation -- the thing everyone gets, with Pro and Elite as upgrades ON TOP of it.

**BEFORE** (what it is now):
- Three equal cards side by side: Free ($0), Pro ($79.99/yr), Elite ($149.99/yr)
- Free card has green border, Pro has orange, Elite has purple
- Each card lists its features independently

**AFTER** (what it should be):
- Top section: "What everyone gets. Forever." -- a large, celebratory block showing ALL free features with checkmarks. This is not a pricing card; it's a value declaration. Make it feel generous.
- Below that: "Want more? Two ways to upgrade." -- Pro and Elite cards side by side
- Pro and Elite cards should show ONLY what they ADD beyond Free (not re-list free features)
- Add a visual "included in Free" badge next to features that free users already have
- Reframe the page: the question isn't "which tier?" -- it's "do I want more than the already-amazing free tier?"
- Keep the toggle for monthly/yearly pricing on the paid tiers

**WHY**: The free tier is Razzle's biggest competitive advantage. No other dynasty tool gives away this much for free. The pricing page should make that obvious. When a visitor sees the pricing page, their reaction should be "wait, all of THIS is free?" -- not "which plan should I pick?" The generous free tier builds trust, reduces decision anxiety, and makes the paid upgrade feel like a luxury rather than a necessity.

### Task 1: Restructure pricing page to lead with the free tier as a celebration
**Accept when**: The pricing page shows a prominent "What everyone gets" section above the paid tier cards. The free features are celebrated, not just listed. Paid tiers show only their incremental additions.
